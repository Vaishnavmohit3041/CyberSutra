Guides

1. Talk Openly About Internet Safety

Open communication is key to ensuring kids are aware of online dangers. Regularly talk to them about topics like cyberbullying, identity theft, and scams. Make sure they know it’s okay to come to you with any concerns or experiences they encounter online. Reinforce that if they ever feel uncomfortable or unsure, they should ask for help.

2. Set Screen Time & Boundaries

Too much screen time can impact a child’s physical and mental health. Set clear boundaries on how much time they can spend on devices each day, and ensure that this includes breaks for physical activity. Encourage a balance by incorporating time for schoolwork, hobbies, and family interaction. Setting these boundaries helps kids develop healthy habits early.

3. Use Kid-Friendly Apps & Sites

Help kids discover educational and safe online resources. Use apps and websites that are specifically designed for their age, with built-in privacy and security features. Encourage creativity, learning, and exploration, but make sure the platforms have a good track record for keeping children safe from inappropriate content.

4. Teach Strong Password Habits

One of the most essential cybersecurity habits is creating strong passwords. Teach kids how to use passwords that are at least 12 characters long, combining letters, numbers, and symbols. Encourage them not to share their passwords with anyone except you. You can make it fun by turning it into a game to create new, unique passwords for different apps and websites!

5. Stay Involved & Curious

Show interest in your child’s online activities. Ask them about the games they play, the videos they watch, and who they interact with online. By staying involved, you’ll be able to detect any potential issues early on. Don’t be afraid to ask questions about online interactions, and engage in their digital world as much as possible.

6. Enable Parental Controls

Parental controls can be a powerful tool for monitoring your child’s online activity. Use built-in controls on devices and apps to filter content, restrict access to certain websites, and set time limits. This ensures that children only access age-appropriate content while allowing you to maintain a sense of control over their digital experience.

7. Spot & Report Phishing

Phishing scams are one of the most common ways hackers trick people into revealing sensitive information. Teach your child to recognize suspicious emails, messages, or pop-ups that ask for personal information. Show them how to spot signs of phishing, such as mismatched URLs or offers that seem “too good to be true.” Encourage them to always report suspicious activity to you.

8. Create a Cyber-Safe Environment

Establish clear rules for safe online behavior and make sure these rules are posted somewhere visible in the house. Use simple and memorable phrases like “Think Before You Click” or “Don’t Share Personal Info Online.” This visual reminder helps keep the concept of cyber safety in their minds and encourages them to make responsible decisions while online.
