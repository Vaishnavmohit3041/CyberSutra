Discussion & Group Activities

1. Cyber Ethics Debate

  • Split the class into teams and assign topics (e.g., privacy vs. security).
  • Give 10 minutes for prep and 5 minutes for presentation.
  • Encourage respectful rebuttals and critical thinking.

2. Group Cyber Scenario Discussion

  • Present a real-life cyber case (e.g., phishing or data leak).
  • Groups discuss what happened, what went wrong, and how to prevent it.
  • Present findings to the class.

3. Digital Footprint Mapping

  • In small groups, list what personal data people share online daily.
  • Create a chart or visual showing how this data spreads.
  • Discuss ways to minimize unnecessary digital footprint.

4. Cyber Safety Brainstorming

  • Pose a problem (e.g., protecting younger siblings online).
  • Groups brainstorm creative, practical solutions.
  • Each group shares ideas and gets peer feedback.

5. Phishing Identification Challenge

  • Provide printed/email samples – some real, some phishing.
  • Groups identify which are fake and why.
  • Discuss common phishing red flags.

6. Cyber Safety Poster Collaboration

  • Each group chooses a cyber safety message.
  • Create a digital or hand-drawn poster with tips and images.
  • Present and hang posters around the school or classroom.

7. Internet Safety Myth Busters

  • Each group gets a common myth (e.g., "Private mode is totally safe").
  • Research and present the truth behind it.
  • Correct misinformation in a fun, informative way.

8. Group Online Privacy Discussion

  • Pose questions like "What info should never be shared online?"
  • Groups discuss and compile a do's and don’ts list.
  • Compare and reflect on different perspectives.

9. Tech-Free Team Challenges

  • Groups go through a day without phones and reflect on the experience.
  • Discuss the role of technology in daily life.
  • Helps students assess screen time habits.

10. Cyberbullying Role Analysis

  • Each group takes on a different role (bully, victim, bystander, school staff).
  • Discuss reactions, feelings, and solutions from each role.
  • Helps build empathy and awareness.
