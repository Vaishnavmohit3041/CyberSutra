Role-Playing Activities

1. Phishing Email Investigator

  • Students role-play as cybersecurity investigators.
  • One team creates fake phishing emails; the other team identifies red flags.
  • Use printed mock emails or slides for realism.

2. Online Stranger Danger

  • One student plays the role of an online stranger.
  • Others practice responses to suspicious or uncomfortable messages.
  • Use role cards to guide behavior.

3. Cyberbullying Scenario Acting

  • Students act out a scene where cyberbullying occurs.
  • Afterward, discuss how it could have been prevented or resolved.
  • Encourages empathy and digital citizenship.

4. Social Media Privacy Settings

  • Students act as influencers managing their privacy settings.
  • Demonstrate what is safe vs unsafe to share online.
  • Use printouts or simulated profiles on slides.

5. Secure or Not?

  • Students are shown examples of websites or apps.
  • They role-play as security auditors evaluating safety.
  • They explain whether they would trust it or not — and why.

6. Cyber Safety Debate

  • Form two groups — for and against a digital topic (like screen time limits).
  • Each side presents arguments in a debate format.
  • Encourages critical thinking and awareness.

7. Safety Superhero Skits

  • Students create superheroes who teach digital safety.
  • Each group acts out a short skit showing how their hero helps.
  • Great for creative expression and teamwork.

8. Red Flag or Green Flag?

  • Use colored cards or stickers.
  • Teacher reads online scenarios; students raise a red or green card.
  • Discuss why the scenario was safe or unsafe.
